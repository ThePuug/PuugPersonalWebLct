<Panel header="Social culture" key="social-culture" itemKey="social-culture">

Many guilds focus on one aspect of the game, or just a tag to wear. We take a different approach — a home away from home for gamers. We cover a wide variety of in-game activities, but what we really strive to be is a community.

Membership means we see you as part of the guild, as a living organism. Events and pick-up groups happen because members take a step forward to organize them. Use <span className="lr-tag-channel">#looking-for-group</span> or hop into voice to see what everyone's up to. Shy or slow to open up? No pressure — but the guild is as great a place as you help make it.

</Panel>

<Panel header="Inclusivity" key="inclusivity" itemKey="inclusivity">

Mutual respect matters to everyone here. We have a zero-tolerance policy for discriminatory behaviour — racism, sexism, homophobia, ableism, abuse, hatred, toxicity. At the same time we don't want anyone walking on glass: everyone's human and we all slip up. Keep jokes in good taste, and bring any concerns to an officer or leader.

</Panel>

<Panel header="Ranks" key="ranks" itemKey="ranks">

Ranks are how we keep track of activity. We don't require you to represent the guild, but we love to acknowledge those who make us their home.

**Rookie** — Where every new member starts, for 7–10 days.

**Member** — Routinely represent, attend events, and chat. Guild stash access and event priority.

**Friend** — Welcome in any capacity without the Member expectations.

**Veteran** — Long-term actives who go above and beyond. Full bank access.

**Officer** — Recruiting, Discord, disputes, guild-building. Interested? Contact @Lucky.

**Ally / Hibernating** — Alliance members, and a resting spot for those away. We never kick for inactivity.

</Panel>

<Panel header="Raiding" key="raiding" itemKey="raiding">

We're not a heavily raid-focused guild, but we have a long history of running raids for the guild, alliance, and pick-up groups. Our veteran leaders have raided since day one and know what makes a raider successful — the goal is to teach not just encounters but the right mentality.

New raiders are welcome to learn the ropes, as long as you'll put in the time: acquire proper gear, practice your class's builds, and respect the difficulty. We run ~2 hours and aim for as many kills as we can.

</Panel>
